Early Life And Education

Lisa Manoban was born initially as Pranpriya Manoban on March 27, 1997, in Bangkok, Thailand. The Thai born later changed her name to Lalisa, meaning praised, on the fortune teller’s advice to bring prosperity. Similarly, Lisa possesses Thai nationality and is of Thai ethnicity.

Career: Who’s that Girl?

From an early age, dancing was the sole passion of our dear idol. Also, Lisa was in the Thai Group ‘We Zaa Cool Kid‘ with her friend Bambam from GOT7. Following her passion for dance and desire to be an idol, Lisa auditioned for ‘YG Entertainment’ at 14. 

Surprisingly, Lisa won the audition in the first place beating 4,000 other applicants. Lisa then shifted to South Korea at the tender age of 14 and became the first non-Korean member to enter YG Entertainment.

On May 10, 2012, YG published a video titled ‘Who’s That Girl?‘ on YouTube, which went viral in Korea. Till now, that video has amassed more than 11 million views.

The video showcased the dancing skill of Lisa without ever revealing her identity. The funny thing was, unlike their other videos, it was first for YG to keep the name secret. Not even her nationality or age was revealed.

After many years of training and waiting, the girl on the video was revealed to be our very own Lisa. With three other members, Jennie, Jisoo, and Rose, Lisa debuted as Blackpink on August 8, 2016. 

Blackpink Is The Revolution

Since its debut, Blackpink has amassed massive success in both the domestic and international Music Industry.

They have achieved considerable feats in Music by being the first girl group in the world to have 27 million subscribers on Youtube, beating Fifth Harmony and Little Mix.

Also, their ‘DDU-Du-DDU-Du‘ became the first Music video by a girl group to cross 800 million views on Youtube.

Apart from that, Blackpink has won many awards in their home, including the ‘New Artist of the Year’ and ‘Bongsang Award‘ at Seoul Music Awards in 2017 and 2018, respectively.

Similarly, they have also won the ‘Gaon Chart Music Awards and ‘Best Music Video ‘ at the ‘Mnet Asian Music Awards‘ in 2016.

Not only that, Blackpink has become the first Korean Girl Group to have a successful World Tour, including Hongkong, Singapore, Japan, Korea, France, Germany, Belgium, Netherlands, USA, Australia, Brazil, and so on.

Following their global influence and marking their World Tour, Blackpink even appeared in numerous American TV shows and Radios, including Late Night with James Corden, Good morning America, etc.

Blackpink also made history by becoming the first girl group from Korea to perform at Coachella (2019), the largest music festival in the World. Just like their concerts, many fans showed support in their jam-packed performance.

What adds charm to this group is the fact that they take good care of their fans. With the intention to be closer to their fans, they have launched their own merchandise too.

Successful Solo Career Of Lisa Manoban

After Jennie and Rose, fans were more than eager to see what the maknae was about to bring to the table. Was it going to be sassy like SOLO or heart-touching like ON THE GROUND? Only time could tell. 

And boy, did she drop the bomb!!!

Yes, after years of waiting, Lisa finally dropped her solo in October, titled ‘LALISA.’ And we’re glad we waited for such a masterpiece. 

Her debut song now has around 453 million views on YouTube alone. It also dethroned Taylor Swift’s single ME! as the most-viewed YouTube music video by a solo artist in 24 hours with 73.6 million views. 

Likewise, her other B-side titled Money also debuted No. 1 on the Rap Digital Song Sales chart. Following that, Miss Manoban also became the first female from the K-pop field to own the roster as stated by Forbes.
