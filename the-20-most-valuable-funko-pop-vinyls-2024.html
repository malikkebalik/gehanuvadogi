The 20 Most Valuable Funko Pop Vinyls (2024)

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, September 27, 2024

What are the most valuable Funko Pop Vinyls?

There are so many of these cool figures on the market, but some are worth considerably more than others.

We’ve put together a list of the 20 most valuable Funko Pops to give you an idea of which ones are worth some serious cash!

All the Funko Pops mentioned on this list have been arranged by estimated value. However, sales are happening all the time, so their values are likely to change.

So, without further ado, here’s a list of the 20 most valuable Funko Pop Vinyls.

Table of Contents

The 20 Most Valuable Funko Pop Vinyls

The list of Funko Pop Vinyls and figures mentioned below have been compiled from various sources around the web, such as Pop Price Guide & Wikipedia.

These are the 20 most valuable Funko Pop Vinyls:

20. Black Ranger Freddy Funko

Cost: $1,510

If power rangers are your thing, or you’re just into collecting all sorts of Funko Pops, then the Black Ranger Funko could be a great addition to your collection.

The Black Ranger Freddy Funko was released at the San Diego Comic-Con in 2017 as a limited batch of twenty-four.

Their exclusivity makes them a lot more desirable, which is why they have an estimated value of $1,510.

However, a Black Ranger Freddy Funko has been known to sell on eBay for $4,300!

19. Batman Freddy Funko (Blue Suit)

Cost: $1,920

Next up, is the Batman Freddy Funko (Blue Suit), which has an estimated price tag of $1,920.

The regular Batman Funko Pop sells for a standard retail price of just $5.00. However, Batman Freddy Funko, in the blue suit, is a lot more desirable to collectors due to its limited numbers.

It has been known for a mint condition Batman Freddy Funko to reach a sale price of around $6,000 on eBay!

18. Bumble Freddy Funko (GITD)

Cost: $2,410

The next Funko Pop on our list is the glow-in-the-dark Bumble Freddy Funko, which has an average price of around $2,410.

Freddy is Funko’s official company mascot, and every so often, the company decides to release a new variation of the Pop, usually where Freddy is mashed together with another character.

Released in 2012 at the San Diego Comic-Con, this rare Funko Pop is based on the abominable snowman Funko, from the 1964 Christmas special, “Rudolph, the Red-Nosed Reindeer.”

It’s estimated to be worth around $2,410, but sellers do try and get a lot more for the glow-in-the-dark Funko Pop Vinyl.

17. Purple Haze Jimi Hendrix (Metallic)

Cost: $2,410

If you’re into Funkos and you love your music, then the Purple Haze Jimi Hendrix Funko has to be part of your collection.

Since it was first released in 2014, its value has been steadily increasing, going from $150 to $2,410.

The limited-edition chase variant of the Jimmi Hendrix Pop is one Funko that’s desired by many and owned by a few.

Keep a lookout on eBay for when the next one of these bad boys pops up!

16. Star Wars: Darth Maul (Holographic)

Cost: $3,020

The holographic Darth Maul Funko Pop is based on the Darth Maul character from Star Wars, and is part of the Star Wars Pop! Line.

It was limited to 480 units, and the Pop was made available exclusively at the 2012 San Diego Comic-Con.

Sales have been very consistent over the years, with an average selling price of around $2,460 and a current estimated value of $3,020.

15. Buzz Lightyear Freddy Funko (Metallic)

Cost: $3,050

If you can manage to find one of these metallic Buzz Lightyear Funko’s, then you’d better get your wallet ready!

The last known sale was in 2016, for an impressive $3,050 on eBay.

It was released in 2011 at the San Diego Comic-Con and is considered to be a highly sort after Pop, hence the limited number of known sales.

So, if you can find one, grab it while you can!

14. Planet Arlia Vegeta

Cost: $3,120

Released in 2014, exclusively to New York Comic-Con/Toy Tokyo, Planet Arlia Vegeta has an estimated value of $3,120.

For all the Dragon Ball Z fans out there, this is one Funko Pop that you’re going to want to pick up as soon as you spot one, as it’s super appealing to Funko Pop collectors around the world.

With that being said, it’s not that rare, so you should be able to grab one on eBay for approximately the same price as mentioned above.

13. Ghost Rider Freddy Funko (Metallic)

Cost: $3,390

The next most valuable Funko Pop Vinyl on our list is the metallic version of Ghost Rider Freddy Funko.

At an estimated $3,390 per Pop, the metallic version of everybody’s favorite motorcycle riding ghost from hell certainly gets your attention.

Ghost Rider Freddy Funko was released in 2013 at the San Diego Comic-Con. Three years later, one sold for around $5,600!

12. Frankenberry Freddy Funko (Metallic)

Cost: $3,430

Next up, we have another Frankenberry Funko, but this one’s the metallic version, so it’s worth a little bit more.

This limited-edition figure was released in 2011 at the San Diego Comic-Con. Unlike the previous glow-in-the-dark version, it features black eyes and a metallic gloss appearance.

Its value has steadily increased over the years, similar to its glow-in-the-dark cousin, from $725 in 2014 to $3,700 in 2018.

Overall, its average value is around $3,340.

11. Tony Stark Freddy Funko (Metallic)

Cost: $3,500

Coming in at number eleven on our list of the most valuable Funko Pop Vinyls is the metallic version of Tony Stark Freddy Funko.

This Funko Pop was released in 2012 exclusively at San Diego Comic-Con and has since been seen to sell for $3,499.

It’s not the rarest Pop Vinyl of the bunch, but if you can pick one up for a reasonable price, then you could be getting a great little investment piece.

10. Boo Berry Funko (GITD)

Cost: $3,540

If glow-in-the-dark Funko Pops are your thing, then the Boo Berry Funko should be right up your street.

Only twenty-four were produced, which is why they’re considered to be pretty desirable, and they were made exclusively available at the 2011 San Diego Comic-Con.

Since it was were released, its value has steadily increased, which is evident on eBay and in private collectors forums.

9. Count Chocula Freddy Funko (Metallic)

Cost: $3,780

Count Chocula Freddy Funko (Metallic) was released exclusively in 2011 at the San Diego Comic-Con.

The Pop is based on General Mills monster-themed breakfast cereal, “Count Chocula,” and is part of Pop! Ad Icons line.

Only 480 units were released, and the value of this little guy has steadily increased over the years, with sales on eBay in 2014 of $725, to its current valuation of $3,780.

8. Boba Fett Freddy Funko (Red Hair)

Cost: $3,990

The Freddy Funko version of Star Wars, Boba Fett droids figure, is worth a lot more than $510, which is the standard price for droid figures retail.

This Funko was released in 2014 at the San Diego Comic-Con, and only twenty-four were made.

Their limited numbers immediately increased their value, with the red-haired version being worth the most.

Boba Fett Freddy Funko is the thirteenth most valuable Funko Pop vinyl.

7. Dumbo (Gold)

Cost: $4,180

The Gold version of the Dumbo Funko Pop commands one hell of a price tag, with an estimated value of $4,180.

Released at the San Diego Comic-Con in 2013, collectors are often on the lookout for this Funko, so it can be a difficult one to find.

After it was released, you could pick one up for around $710 in 2014, however, fast forward to 2020, and you’re now looking at about $6,000!

However, that’s for a one-off purchase; the average is still around $4,180. But, it just goes to show that if people are willing to pay, then the sky’s the limit.

6. Buzz Lightyear Freddy Funko (GITD)

Cost: $5,030

Coming in at number six is the Buzz Lightyear Freddy Funko (GITD), with an estimated value of $5,030.

This Funko Pop was released exclusively at the 2011 San Diego Comic-Con and was modeled on the loveable astronaut from the Toy Story movies.

Since its release, Buzz Lightyear Freddy Funko (GITD) Pops have sold for up to $6,500, but the average value is around $5,030.

If the trend continues in the direction it’s heading, the Buzz Lightyear Freddy Funko (GITD) could hit the $10,000 mark!

5. Frankenberry Freddy Funko (GITD)

Cost: $5,610

Funko decided to play around with their mascot once again and came up with the Frankenberry Freddy Funko (GITD).

Frankenberry was released in 2011 at the San Diego Comic-Con and has managed to climb up the value ladder, from $725 to $5,610.

It’s no surprise that this Funko has been able to reach these impressive amounts, as the Frankenberry Pop is unique and well-loved within the Funko community, And, most collectors love to grab the all the different variations of the companies mascot.

4. Stan Lee Signed Pops (Metallic)

Cost: $6,100

If you’ve got the cash to spend, then this is perhaps one of the better investments on the list.

Since his passing in 2018, anything with Stan Lee’s “signature” on it will most likely have increased in value, and Funko Pops are no exception.

A set of gold and chrome metallic Stan Lee Pops were listed on eBay for an astronomical $100,000, and are still yet to sell.

However, similar Pops have been known to sell for $6,100.

3. Dumbo (Clown)

Cost: $7,430

Coming in at number three is the Dumbo (Clown) Funko Pop Vinyl.

It’s based on a scene in Disney’s 1941 film, “Dumbo,” where the elephant is wearing clown makeup and forced to perform in a circus.

The Pop was released at the 2013 San Diego Comic-Con. It has an estimated value of $7,430, based on previous sales, and it’s one you’ll not see all that often, that’s for sure. 

2. Jaime Lannister Freddy Funko (Bloody)

Cost: $9,310

If you’re into Game of Thrones, then you’ll appreciate this Funko Pop.

It’s a “bloody” variant of Jaime Lannister from Game of Thrones and was released exclusively at the 2013 San Diego Comic-Con.

The Pop is currently worth an estimated $9,310; however, listings on eBay have sold for $13,500 in the past, so it wouldn’t be a surprise to see one go for more than $20,000.

1. Alex DeLarge Clockwork Orange Funko (GITD)

Cost: $13,300

The most valuable Funko Pop is the glow-in-the-dark Alex DeLarge Clockwork Orange Funko.

The main difference between this version of Alex DeLarge Clockwork Orange and the standard versions is that he isn’t holding his cane. It’s such a small difference but does add to its increasing value.

Now, unlike other Pops on the list, its estimated value of $13,300 is pretty legit, as others have reportedly sold on eBay for around the $13,000 mark, back in 2016 and 2017.

So, if you do manage to find one for a reasonable price, snap it up, as it’s likely to keep going up in value!

Considering there are thousands of Funko Pops on the market, it’s interesting to see all the little details that make some a lot more valuable than others.
